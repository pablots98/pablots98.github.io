Pablo Tejero

Hi, I’m Pablo Tejero, a biologist with a passion for understanding complex biological systems through computational approaches. My academic journey began with a Bachelor's degree in Biology, followed by a Master's in teaching high school biology. However, my curiosity for the intersection of biology and technology led me to Maastricht, where I completed a two-year Master's in Systems Biology.

During this time, I dove deep into bioinformatics, network biology, computational biology, and modeling. My 7-month internship allowed me to explore metabolic context-specific models, where I evaluated thresholding algorithms and identified core reactions for senescent fibroblasts. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience that solidified my love for working at the crossroads of biology and data.

Alongside my studies, I had the opportunity to contribute to Maastricht University as a student ambassador, helping prospective students, and as a social media ambassador, sharing insights into student life. I also took on the role of a tutor for a Bachelor's course, a position that allowed me to further develop my communication and mentoring skills.

I’m proficient in Python, R, and MATLAB, which I use to tackle complex biological problems. Looking ahead, I am excited about the possibilities in personalized medicine, particularly where computational biology and bioinformatics can make a significant impact. I aim to contribute to this field by developing models and tools that help tailor medical treatments to individual patients.

Feel free to reach out if you’d like to discuss potential collaborations, research opportunities, or any projects where biology meets data!
